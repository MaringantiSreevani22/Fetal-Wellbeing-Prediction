Fetal development is a complex process that begins at conception and continues until birth, typically over the course of about 40 weeks. This period is divided into three main stages, each characterized by specific developmental milestones:

1. Germinal Stage (Weeks 1-2)
- Fertilization: The sperm and egg combine to form a single-celled zygote, which contains all the genetic material necessary for the formation of a human.
- Cell Division and Implantation: The zygote undergoes rapid cell division as it travels down the fallopian tube, forming a cluster of cells known as a blastocyst. Around the end of the first week, the blastocyst implants itself into the uterine wall, where it continues to develop.
- During this stage, the placenta and amniotic sac start to form, providing protection and nutrient exchange for the developing embryo.

2. Embryonic Stage (Weeks 3-8)
- Organ Formation (Organogenesis): This is a critical period when the basic structures of organs and systems, like the heart, brain, spinal cord, and digestive system, begin to form. By the end of the third week, the heart starts beating.
- Neural Tube Formation: Around week four, the neural tube (which later becomes the brain and spinal cord) begins to develop.
- Formation of Limbs and Facial Features: Limb buds appear, and facial features, including the eyes, ears, and mouth, begin to form.
- This stage is particularly vulnerable to teratogens (substances that can cause birth defects), as organs are forming and highly susceptible to external influences.

3. Fetal Stage (Weeks 9-40)
- Growth and Maturation: During this stage, the fetus undergoes rapid growth, and organs and systems mature to prepare for life outside the womb. The fetus starts to look more human, with defined fingers, toes, and facial features.
- Movement and Reflexes: By around week 16-20, the mother can often feel fetal movements. The fetus begins to develop reflexes like sucking and swallowing.
- Viability and Lung Development: By about 24 weeks, the fetus reaches a stage known as "viability," meaning it may survive outside the womb with medical support. Lung development is crucial here, and the lungs mature throughout the remainder of the pregnancy.
- Final Weeks: The fetus gains more weight, and its body systems prepare for independent function. The brain undergoes significant development, which continues after birth.

Milestones in Fetal Development:
- First Trimester (Weeks 1-12): Major organs begin to form, and by the end of the trimester, the fetus is about 3 inches long.
- Second Trimester (Weeks 13-26): The fetus grows in size, and physical movements become more pronounced. This trimester often allows detailed ultrasound imaging of the fetus's development.
- Third Trimester (Weeks 27-40): The fetus gains weight, its organs fully mature, and it prepares for birth. The brain and lungs are among the last organs to complete development.

Fetal development is a highly organized and precise process, where each stage relies on the previous one to lay the foundation for a healthy baby. Advances in prenatal care, such as ultrasound imaging and maternal health monitoring, allow for better observation and understanding of this intricate process, enabling early intervention in cases of abnormalities.
