Fetal Development Stage: Pathological Findings

"Pathological" findings indicate potential abnormalities or complications in fetal development. If your results show "Pathological" signs, immediate attention is required to ensure the health of both mother and baby. Common pathological conditions include:

  • Intrauterine Growth Restriction (IUGR)
    Signs: Decreased fetal movement, low amniotic fluid
    Risks: Premature birth, low birth weight
  • Fetal Distress
    Signs: Abnormal fetal heart rate, reduced movement
    Risks: Stillbirth, premature birth
  • Placental Abruption
    Signs: Abdominal pain, vaginal bleeding
    Risks: Premature birth, neonatal complications
  • Oligohydramnios (Low Amniotic Fluid)
    Signs: Low fluid levels, limited fetal movement
    Risks: Premature rupture of membranes, birth defects
  • Preterm Labor
    Signs: Regular contractions, pelvic pressure
    Risks: Premature birth, developmental delays
  • Infections
    Signs: Fever, unusual discharge
    Risks: Preterm labor, low birth weight

What to Do:

  • Consult a healthcare provider for further testing and care.
  • Monitor fetal health with ultrasounds and stress tests.
  • Follow prescribed treatments to manage conditions and reduce risks.
